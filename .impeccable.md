## Design Context

### Users
Stylora targets SaaS development teams first.

They need a credible browser workspace for day-to-day PostgreSQL work, with a structure that supports organizations and projects from the start. They may want to self-host the product now or move to a managed cloud offer later.

### Brand Personality
Stylora should feel editorial, premium, and technically credible.

The emotional goal is confidence and satisfaction: the product should feel complete, serious, and pleasant to use every day, not like a stripped-down admin tool.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a light-first, product-first visual direction with strong typography and restrained color.

Avoid neon dark AI aesthetics, generic corporate B2B gradients, and interchangeable SaaS landing page patterns. The site should feel crafted, calm, and distinctive, with subtle references to database structures, workspaces, and tabbed tools.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with product credibility, not startup hype.
2. Show that Stylora is PostgreSQL-first today without overpromising future backends.
3. Make the experience feel more complete and better structured than typical web database tools.
4. Keep the marketing voice precise, understated, and product-oriented.
5. Use typography, composition, and restraint to create memorability instead of flashy effects.
